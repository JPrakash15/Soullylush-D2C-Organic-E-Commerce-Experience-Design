02 — Overview

What is
Soullylush?

Soullylush is a farm-to-home organic brand selling protein shakes, diapers, gummy vitamins, snacks, and skin care — certified by NPOP, FSSAI, ECOCERT, and Earth Seer.

The design challenge: make organic grocery feel as intuitive and emotionally resonant as walking into a trusted neighbourhood store — at scale, across two very different interaction paradigms.

Design Mandate

"Organic grocery shoppers are high-trust, low-impulse buyers. Every design decision must reduce anxiety and build confidence — not just look good."

03 — Problem Statement

The trust-discovery
gap in organic
D2C commerce

India's organic grocery market is growing rapidly, but faces a persistent credibility problem. Customers are willing to pay a premium — but they drop off at two critical moments.

Awareness Drop

"Is this brand actually organic — or just marketing?" Users leave before they reach certifications buried at the bottom of the page.

Conversion Drop

"Can I find what I need quickly and trust this checkout?" Ambiguous UX patterns in cart and checkout lose buyers at the final step.

Three Core Design Challenges
01
Trust-building at scale
Communicating authenticity and certifications without overwhelming visual hierarchy or burying them below the fold.
02
Multi-platform cohesion
Delivering a seamless brand experience across a full-width web storefront and a compact mobile app — two fundamentally different interaction paradigms.
03
Conversion optimisation
Guiding users from product discovery to checkout with minimal friction, in a category where impulse purchase is low and consideration is high.

Checkout Screen

Single-page checkout. Full cost transparency before payment. Shipping tier comparison as card selection.

Navigation Architecture

5-tab bottom nav: a retention-first decision

The Reorder and Track tabs live at the navigation level — not buried in an account menu. This is a D2C retention pattern that surfaces post-purchase behaviour as primary actions. Most apps treat reorder as a secondary feature. Soullylush treats it as a first-class destination.

Home Explore Track ★ Reorder ★ Cart
Cart UX — Ambiguity Risk

Checkbox cart: a non-standard pattern that needs clearer affordance

The cart uses a checkbox model suggesting items can be selected/deselected before checkout — potentially useful for grocery list management, but dangerously unclear. Users may not understand unchecked items won't proceed.

Fix (P0): Either remove checkboxes (standard cart) or add persistent micro-copy: "Uncheck items to save for later." No UX pattern should require explanation — but if it does, provide it inline.

08 — Iteration Roadmap

Good design ships.
Great design iterates.

This isn't a list of what went wrong. It's a prioritised brief for what comes next. Every product designer worth hiring knows that v1 is the start of a conversation, not the end.

P0
Critical — Fix Before Launch
Move certifications above the fold
Add a compact trust strip directly below the hero CTA. Research shows 15–25% conversion lift for organic D2C brands.
Default checkout to Standard shipping
Pre-selecting Priority (+₹20) inflates perceived cost. Make the upgrade a clear opt-in, not the default state.
Clarify cart checkbox behaviour
Add "Uncheck to save for later" micro-copy, or revert to standard cart. No UX pattern should silently fail.
Show total inside payment CTA
"Proceed to Payment — ₹633" confirms the amount before the tap. Eliminates a common hesitation moment.
P1
High Impact — Next Sprint
Unify CTA colour system
Replace orange '+cart' buttons with brand green on mobile. One primary CTA colour. No exceptions.
Make flavour thumbnails interactive
Tapping a colour swatch on PDP should swap the hero image. Currently they appear decorative — a missed affordance.
Subscribe & Save in rupees
"Save ₹60/month" outperforms "Save 10%" in D2C subscription conversion. Concrete numbers beat percentages.
Show all categories on desktop
Carousel arrows on a desktop viewport suggest hidden content and reduce engagement. Full row on wide screens.
Direct cart add on web listings
'View Details' CTA removes a conversion step for decisive buyers. Add '+Cart' directly on product listing cards.
P2
Improvement — Backlog
Blog category tags + email capture
Add Nutrition/Recipes/Lifestyle tags for self-segmentation. Email capture adjacent to blog converts traffic to owned audience.
Vary testimonial star ratings
Identical 4.5-star ratings across all reviews reduces believability. Varied, authentic social proof converts better.
Login CTA refinement
The house icon on 'Continue' is metaphorically ambiguous. Replace with arrow or explicit 'Get OTP' label.
Fix Explore tile grid
Originals tile should be a full-width banner above a consistent grid — not an irregular double-width cell.
Performance optimisation
Isometric farm illustration → SVG or Lottie. Maintains visual quality while dramatically improving page load speed.

10 — Designer Reflection

Design must be
convertible,
not just visible.

Designing Soullylush reinforced a core belief: a beautiful e-commerce interface that doesn't guide the user to 'Add to Cart' is a failed design — regardless of how polished it looks.

This project deepened my appreciation for category-specific design constraints. Organic grocery is not fashion e-commerce or SaaS. The purchase psychology is slower, the trust barriers are higher, and the emotional payoff is long-term health, not instant gratification. Every section had to work harder to answer one implicit question: "Can I trust this brand with my family's health?"

The dual-platform challenge was equally instructive. The web homepage needed to tell a complete brand story. The mobile app assumed higher intent — the user wants to complete a task, not read a mission statement. Designing for these two mindsets simultaneously, while maintaining visual and behavioural coherence, required continuous calibration between storytelling and efficiency.

Design Philosophy
"Colour, typography, and visual style are not decoration — they create the emotional context within which a user decides whether to trust a brand."
Every green on Soullylush communicates health, nature, and permission. Every orange communicates harvest energy and urgency.
The Soullylush design isn't a skin over a product catalogue. It is the brand's first — and sometimes only — conversation with every customer.
What I would do differently
01
Start with a usability test of the cart checkout flow — the checkbox ambiguity would have surfaced in 5 minutes of testing.
02
Map the certification placement earlier in the IA phase, not after the homepage layout was set.
03
Define CTA colour rules in the design system before screen design — not discovered as an inconsistency after.
